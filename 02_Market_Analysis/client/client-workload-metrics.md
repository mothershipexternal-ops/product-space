**Segment framework:** See [`../market/segment-definitions.md`](../market/segment-definitions.md) — canonical Solo (1–4), Small (5–9), Mid (10–19), Growth (20–49) definitions.

The number of **simultaneous (active) projects** and the **total projects per month/year** for an average **specialty contractor firm** (e.g., electrical, plumbing, HVAC, roofing, drywall, or other trade subcontractors) varies **widely** based on factors like:

- Firm size (small 1-5 person shop vs. mid-size 10-50 employees vs. larger regional player)
- Trade type (service-oriented like plumbing/HVAC often juggle more smaller jobs; larger trades like electrical or concrete on big commercial builds handle fewer but longer ones)
- Project mix (residential service/repairs/remodels vs. new commercial construction vs. public/institutional)
- Location and market (e.g., high-demand areas like Raleigh, NC, might see more volume)
- Crew/labor availability and scheduling

There's no single "official" statistic from sources like BLS or industry reports for exact averages on project counts (they track employment, revenue, etc., more than job volume), but industry discussions, contractor forums, and management benchmarks provide a realistic picture.

### Simultaneous (Active/Overlapping) Projects by Segment

| Segment | Employees | Active Projects | Invoices/Month (AP) | Primary procurement initiator |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Solo** | 1–4 | 2–4 | 15–40 | Owner (directly) |
| **Small** | 5–9 | 3–7 | 30–80 | Field tech → owner |
| **Mid** | 10–19 | 5–12 | 75–150 | Field tech → PM (informal) |
| **Growth** | 20–49 | 8–20 | 150–400+ | Field tech → PM (should be formal PO) |

For an **average specialty contractor firm** (typically small to mid-size, which makes up most of the ~hundreds of thousands of specialty trade firms in the US):

- **Solo firms** (1–4 employees, owner-operator): Typically **2–4 active projects**. High overlap of service calls vs. project work. The owner personally manages every order.
- **Small firms** (5–9 employees, owner + field crew): Often **3–7 active projects** at once. Many juggle quick service calls/repairs alongside 1–2 larger installs/remodels.
- **Mid-size firms** (10–19 employees): Typically **5–12 active jobs** simultaneously. A PM or senior tech is starting to emerge; owner still involved in material calls.
- **General rule from construction management sources**: Effective PMs in specialty trades handle **2–5 projects** at a time without major issues (similar to broader construction advice of 1–3 for GCs, but subs can stretch higher due to shorter scopes and more crew delegation). Beyond 5–6 per PM often leads to dropped balls, delays, or quality slips.

Examples by trade:
- **Plumbing/HVAC** (service-heavy): Higher overlap possible—many small jobs (repairs, installs) mean crews rotate daily/weekly, so firm-wide 5–15+ active at peak.
- **Electrical**: Often **3–8** active, with PMs managing 2–4 larger ones.
- **Roofing or concrete**: Fewer (2–6), as jobs are weather-dependent and crew-intensive.

Overloading (e.g., 10+ per PM) is common in busy markets but risky—leads to burnout, errors, or safety issues.

### Total Projects per Month/Year
This is even more variable, as "projects" range from tiny service calls ($500–$2k, 1 day) to full-system installs ($20k–$200k+, weeks/months) to large commercial subcontracts ($100k–$1M+, months).

Rough industry norms by segment:
- **Solo (1–4 emp)**: **10–30+ jobs/mo** if service-focused; **3–8/mo** if project-focused. Owner completes 80–200+ jobs/year. Each job has 1–5 AP invoices → 15–40 invoices/mo.
- **Small (5–9 emp)**: **5–15 jobs/mo** completed/started. 30–80 AP invoices/mo. Owner spends 5–10 hrs/wk on admin.
- **Mid (10–19 emp, canonical name)**: **8–25 jobs/mo**. 75–150 AP invoices/mo. Part-time bookkeeper 15+ hrs/wk on AP.
- **Growth (20–49 emp, canonical name)**: **15–50+ jobs/mo**. 150–400+ AP invoices/mo. Full-time controller. At $42/invoice manual processing cost → **$6,300–$16,800/mo in AP labor** for this segment.

Revenue correlation: **Small** firms ($0.5–2M revenue) → 50–150 jobs/yr. **Mid** firms ($2–5M) → 40–100 jobs/yr (fewer but larger). **Growth** firms ($5–15M) → 80–200+ jobs/yr with multiple crews.

In high-volume trades like HVAC/plumbing (with lots of maintenance/repairs), annual job counts skew higher. In trades tied to big builds (e.g., structural steel, major electrical), lower counts but bigger tickets.

**Conduit implication:** Invoice volume drives COGS linearly (~$0.022–$0.035/text or photo processed). A Growth segment customer at 300 invoices/mo has ~$10.50/mo COGS — yielding 96%+ gross margin at $299/mo. The Mid segment at 100 invoices/mo has ~$3.50/mo COGS at $99/mo (Starter) — 96.5% gross margin.
