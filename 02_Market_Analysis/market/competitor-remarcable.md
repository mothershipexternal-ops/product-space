# Competitor Deep Dive: Remarcable

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-17
**Category:** Direct competitor — trade contractor procurement, Three-Way Match

---

## Overview

Remarcable is a cloud-based material procurement and management platform purpose-built for construction trade contractors, primarily electrical, with announced 2026 expansion into mechanical (HVAC/plumbing). It centralizes the entire procurement lifecycle: material requisitions, purchase orders, supplier quoting, warehouse inventory, tool tracking, and accounting integration.

Its core promise is unifying field crews, office staff, prefab shops, warehouses, and suppliers on one platform.

## Product & Workflow

### How It Works

1. Field tech submits a material, tool, or prefab requisition via the Remarcable mobile app (iOS, catalog of 175,000+ products)
2. PM or purchasing dept reviews, approves, and issues POs directly to preferred suppliers via 450+ supplier API/EDI connections (Graybar, Sonepar, Wesco, Rexel)
3. Supplier delivers; packing slip/delivery receipt is logged
4. Automated Three-Way Match: PO vs. delivery receipt vs. supplier invoice — discrepancies flagged automatically
5. Reconciled data pushed to accounting software (QuickBooks Desktop, QuickBooks Online, Foundation Software)
6. In-platform messaging connects field, office, warehouse, and supplier

### Key Features

- 175,000+ product catalog with real-time pricing and availability
- 450+ supplier integrations (API/EDI — Graybar, Sonepar, Wesco, Rexel)
- Digital RFQs and competitive quoting
- Automated Three-Way Match (PO + delivery + invoice)
- Warehouse and inventory management
- Tool tracking
- Job costing and cost code assignment
- 25 accounting system integrations (QBD, QBO, Foundation Software)
- In-platform supplier and internal messaging

## Pricing

**Not publicly disclosed.** Sales-led, custom enterprise pricing. No self-serve tiers. No pricing visible without a sales call. This is a structural signal that the product is not designed for the 5–50 employee market — small shops cannot evaluate it without committing to a sales process.

---

## QuickBooks Integration

Supports both QuickBooks Desktop (primary, most documented) and QuickBooks Online. The Three-Way Match reconciliation happens *inside Remarcable* before data is pushed to QBO — meaning QBO receives clean, matched data. Foundation Software also integrated.

AutoSync mode available for QBD: two-step PO/invoice import, batch processing.

---

## How Field Workers Interact

Field workers must:
1. Download the Remarcable iOS app (iOS 17.6+ required)
2. Create and log into an account
3. Browse the product catalog and build a cart
4. Submit a formal requisition request

**This is a screen-first, app-dependent workflow.** It requires iOS 17.6+ (excludes older iPhones and Android users), account creation, and UI familiarity. **Directly violates the "Field First" design principle — field workers must login.**

No SMS-native workflow. No text-to-request capability.

## Key Weaknesses vs. Conduit

1. **App dependency is the core friction point.** Field techs must download an app, create an account, and learn a UI. Conduit's zero-app SMS workflow is a direct counter to this.

2. **Wrong company size fit.** Remarcable's installed base is large commercial contractors. A 10-person HVAC shop is too small to merit a sales conversation, too small to navigate enterprise onboarding, and likely priced out.

3. **Opaque pricing prevents self-serve adoption.** Small contractors cannot simply sign up and start — they need a demo and a contract.

4. **iOS 17.6+ requirement** excludes Android users and older iPhones common among field workers.

5. **Different purchasing paradigm.** Remarcable targets companies *with* a dedicated purchasing department. Conduit targets companies *where the PM is* the purchasing department.

---

## Positioning Statement Against Remarcable

> "Remarcable is for contractors with a purchasing department. Conduit is for contractors *who are* the purchasing department."
