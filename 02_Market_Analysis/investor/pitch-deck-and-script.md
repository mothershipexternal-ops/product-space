## The 2:30 Pitch Script

**[Slide 1]** Hi, I'm Dmitry Krasnov, founder of Conduit. That split screen is the product: a field text on the left, a QuickBooks purchase order on the right. We close that gap for trade contractors.

**[Slide 2]** Trade contractors run on 3–5% net margins — half of a typical small business. Despite those thin margins, field crews order materials every day by texting supply houses from job sites. No purchase order. No record.

**[Slide 3]** Without a PO, invoices can't be verified. Industry data puts 22% of invoices in exception handling before payment — construction skews worse. The table shows how the cost compounds by firm size. For a 15-person electrical contractor, it's $90,000 a year.

**[Slide 4]** Our product sits between the field and the back office. The crew texts a photo or a materials list to our number — no app, no login. We extract line items, normalize trade terminology, and push a 3-way matched purchase order directly into QuickBooks. The field workflow doesn't change. The office gets a complete audit trail.

**[Slide 5]** Enterprise tools like Kojo require implementation budgets small shops don't have. Generic SMB tools have no job costing. The gap: fewer than 10% of 5–50-employee contractors use any specialized procurement tool.

**[Slide 6]** Our market is specialty trade contractors: electrical, plumbing, HVAC. 130,000 firms in the 5–49-employee range, 110,000 on QuickBooks. We're targeting $7.3 million in ARR at 3.3% SAM penetration by Year 3.

**[Slide 7]** Pricing runs $49 to $299 a month. The Pro tier at $299 saves a 15-person electrical contractor $90,000 a year — 30 times the subscription cost. We're raising a $1.1 million seed round — 18 months to $1 million in core ARR.

**[Slide 8]** Core product ships Q3 2026. First paying customers via the QBO App Store, Q1 2027. Back-office expansion in Q3 2027. Native field app and supplier network in 2028.

**[Slide 9]** We have the technical background to build the AI and direct experience with the construction back-office workflows this product has to serve.

**[Slide 10]** Scan the QR code to watch a live demo — a real field text becoming a matched purchase order in QuickBooks. Thank you.

*Timing: 280 words · 2:10 at 130 WPM. Add the ask as the last line you say before the room reacts — do not rush past it.*

## Investor Q&A Prep: The NAICS 238 vs. 750K Question

Investors who have seen earlier versions of this pitch may ask: "I've seen 750,000 firms mentioned — now you're saying 130,000. What changed?"

**Answer:**
> "The 750,000 figure includes all U.S. construction firms with employees — that's NAICS 23, which includes general contractors, homebuilders, and highway construction. Our product solves a specific problem for specialty trade contractors — the electricians, plumbers, HVAC techs, and roofers. That's NAICS 238. The Census Bureau counts roughly 130,000 firms with 5–49 employees in that specific category. The 750K was a common shorthand in the industry, but using it without the NAICS qualifier is technically wrong. We've corrected it."

**Why the smaller number is stronger:**
- 3.3% SAM penetration to reach $7.26M ARR is achievable and comparable to Field Materials' early trajectory
- The smaller, specific TAM signals domain expertise and builds trust with construction-tech-savvy investors
- The unit economics (47:1 LTV:CAC for Growth segment at $150 CAC) are the real story — not the TAM size
