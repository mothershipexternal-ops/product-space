# User Research Guide: The Office Manager (Persona: "The Chaos Tamer")

**Goal:** Understand the manual workflow friction and validate QBO integration needs.

---

## 1. Persona Summary & Motivation
*   **Role:** Office Manager / Bookkeeper / Admin / Spouse of Owner.
*   **Typical Size:** The sole admin for a 5-20 person crew.
*   **Pain Point:** Chaos. Chasing receipts, nagging field staff, data entry errors.
*   **Motivation:** "Stop the madness." Save time. Go home at 5 PM.
*   **Fear:** Looking incompetent to the owner; messing up the books; tax audits.

---

## 2. Recruiting Strategy (Where to find them)
*   **Facebook Groups:** "Bookkeepers for Construction", "QuickBooks Users Group".
*   **Local Meetups:** "QuickBooks User Groups" or admin networking events.
*   **The "Sympathy" Hook:** "Hi [Name], I'm building a tool to help stop field crews from losing receipts. I know how much of a nightmare that is for the office. Can I ask how you handle it today?"

---

## 3. The Interview Script (The "Mom Test" Approach)

### Phase 1: The Work (Empathy)
*   "Your job sounds incredibly busy. What does a typical Monday morning look like for you?"
*   "How many invoices do you process in a week? 10? 100?"
*   "What's the most annoying part of your week? Be honest."

### Phase 2: The Process (The Workflow)
*   **The Chase:** "Walk me through what happens when an invoice comes in from Graybar but you don't have a PO or a packing slip. What do you do?"
    *   *Follow-up:* "Do you call the project manager? Do you text the owner? How often does that happen?"
*   **The Match:** "How do you verify the prices? do you have a price list, or do you just trust the vendor?"
*   **The Entry:** "How long does it take you to enter a stack of invoices into QuickBooks? Do you do it line-by-line or just the total?"
*   **Job Costing:** "How important is it to tag every expense to a specific 'Customer:Project' in QuickBooks? Does the owner nag you about that?"

### Phase 3: The Tools (Friction)
*   **QuickBooks:** "What do you hate most about QuickBooks Online? What is the one thing you wish it did better?"
*   **Field Communication:** "Do the guys text you orders? Send photos? Call you? Which method do you prefer / hate the most?"
*   **Receipts:** "Do you use Dext, Receipt Bank, or Hubdoc? Why or why not?" (Crucial to know why existing tools fail).

### Phase 4: The "Magic Wand"
*   "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one part of the ordering process, what would it be?"
    *   *Prompt:* "Would it be the guys actually submitting the order? Or would it be the data entry into QuickBooks?"

---

## 4. Red Flags & Green Lights

| Signal | Meaning | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **"I just pay whatever the statement says."** | **RED FLAG.** Low sophistication. They don't care about matching. | **Disqualify.** |
| **"The owner handles all the ordering."** | **RED FLAG.** Too small (Micro-business). | **Disqualify.** |
| **"I spend every Friday calling foremen to ask what they bought."** | **GREEN LIGHT.** High pain. High friction. Perfect user. | **Primary Prospect.** |
| **"I hate typed data entry. I just want to review and click 'approve'."** | **GREEN LIGHT.** Validate our "AI Draft" feature. | **Ideal Customer.** |

---

## 5. Post-Interview Analysis
*   **What is their current "Hack"?** (e.g., Shared Excel sheet, whiteboard, WhatsApp group).
*   **How sophisticated is their QBO usage?** (Do they use Projects? Classes? Locations?).
*   **Who is the real decision maker for software?** (Usually the Owner, but the Manager is the *Influencer*).
