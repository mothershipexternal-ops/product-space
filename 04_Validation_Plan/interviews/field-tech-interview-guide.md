# User Research Guide: The Field Tech (Persona: "The Resister")

**Goal:** Validate the "Text-First" interaction model and identify friction points.

---

## 1. Persona Summary & Motivation
*   **Role:** Foreman / Lead Technician / Expeditor.
*   **Typical User:** "Blue Collar" pro. Hands-on. Values speed and skill.
*   **Pain Point:** "Stupid apps." Doesn't want to be an admin.
*   **Motivation:** "Get the material so I can finish the job and go home."
*   **Fear:** Being tracked; looking dumb with technology; wasting time on "office work".

---

## 2. Recruiting Strategy (Where to find them)
*   **The Supply House Parking Lot:** This is the best place. Mornings (6:30-7:30 AM).
*   **Reddit:** r/electricians, r/plumbing. Posting a thread: "What's the worst app your boss makes you use?"
*   **Job Sites:** If you know an owner, ask to visit a site during lunch break. Bring donuts.

---

## 3. The Interview Script (The "Mom Test" Approach)

### Phase 1: The Reality (Breaking the Ice)
*   "I'm not a boss and I'm not selling anything. I just want to know why construction software sucks." (They will laugh and agree).
*   "What's your trade? How long have you been in the field?"

### Phase 2: The Workflow (Current Habits)
*   **The Order:** "You're on a job site and realize you're out of 12-2 wire. What do you do right now? Walk me through the exact steps."
    *   *Listen for:* "I call the supply house", "I text my boss", "I write it on a piece of drywall".
*   **The Receipt:** "When the delivery truck shows up, what happens to the packing slip? Be honest—where does it go?"
*   **The Returns:** "What happens if they send you the wrong part? How do you return it? Is that a hassle?"

### Phase 3: The Friction (Why Apps Fail)
*   "Has your company ever tried to make you use an app for this? Like an iPad thing?"
*   "What did you hate about it? Was it slow? Too many clicks? Hard to see in the sun?"
*   "If you could just text a picture of a list to a number and have it magically appear at the shop, would you do that? Or is texting still a pain?"

### Phase 4: The "Shadow IT"
*   "Do you guys have a WhatsApp group or a group text for the crew? What do you talk about in there?"
*   "Do you ever take photos of parts to show the supply house what you need? Can I see your camera roll?" (Looking for photos of labels, broken parts, scribbled lists).

---

## 4. Red Flags & Green Lights

| Signal | Meaning | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **"I love using the iPad for everything."** | **RED FLAG (Sort of).** They might be fine with Procore/Kojo. Less need for our "Invisible" app. | **Note.** |
| **"I don't have a smartphone / I have a flip phone."** | **RED FLAG.** Rare, but fatal for our model. | **Disqualify.** |
| **"I just call Mike at Graybar. He knows what I need."** | **YELLOW FLAG.** High relationship dependency. Hard to automate "Mike". | **Challenge:** "What if Mike is on vacation?" |
| **"I hate the office lady nagging me for receipts. I lose them all the time."** | **GREEN LIGHT.** Acute pain. They want a solution to get the office off their back. | **Ideal User Validity.** |

---

## 5. Post-Interview Analysis
*   **Did they show you their phone?** (Photos of parts/receipts validate the vision AI feature).
*   **How fast do they type?** (If they type with one finger, Voice-to-Text is critical).
*   **What is their "literacy" with tech?** (Do they use other apps? TikTok? Facebook?).
