# User Research Guide: The Business Owner (Persona: "The Profit Hawk")

**Goal:** Determine if they care enough about "material leakage" to pay for a solution.
**Segment framework:** See [`../../02_Market_Analysis/market/segment-definitions.md`](../../02_Market_Analysis/market/segment-definitions.md) — canonical Solo (1–4), Small (5–9), Mid (10–19), Growth (20–49) definitions.

---

## 0. Segment Screener (Ask First — Before Any Other Questions)

**The most important question in this guide is the first one.** The owner's segment (Solo/Small/Mid/Growth) determines which version of the problem they have and which Conduit tier applies. Ask this before anything else, and adjust follow-up depth accordingly.

**Screener question:**
> "Before we start — how many people work at your company, including yourself?"

**Interpretation:**
| Answer | Segment | Their version of the problem | Relevant Conduit tier |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1–4 people | **Solo** | Owner IS field worker; receipt lost in glovebox; no one to reconcile against | $49/mo Solo tier — receipt capture focus |
| 5–9 people | **Small** | Field crew texts owner; owner calls supplier while driving; packing slip in van | $99/mo Starter — text-to-PO focus |
| 10–19 people | **Mid** | Emerging PM but not clean; bookkeeper reconciling manually; ProAdvisor may be in the mix | $139 blended / Starter→Pro transition |
| 20–49 people | **Growth** | Multiple crews; dedicated controller; highest dollar leakage; 3-way match needed | $299/mo Pro — full 3-way match focus |

**Note for Solo owners:** The chaos cycle is truncated. There's no PM to skip the PO — there IS no PM. Focus interview questions on receipt capture, glovebox/pocket receipts, and job costing for tax purposes (not PO workflow). Solo owners may not feel the "blind payment" problem the same way because they personally placed the order — they just can't prove which job it was for.

## 1. Persona Summary & Motivation
*   **Role:** Owner / President of a Trade Contractor firm (Electrical, HVAC, Plumbing).
*   **Typical Size:** 5-49 Employees.
*   **Pain Point:** Profit Leakage. They hate paying for materials that never get installed or billed.
*   **Motivation:** "Stop losing money."
*   **Fear:** Being ripped off by employees or suppliers; losing control as the company grows.

---

## 2. Recruiting Strategy (Where to find them)
*   **LinkedIn Search:** Filter by "Owner" + Industry "Construction" + Company Size "11-50".
*   **Local Chambers of Commerce:** Look for members of local builder associations.
*   **Facebook Groups:** "MEP Contractor Business Owners", "Blue Collar Millionaires".
*   **The "Ego" Hook:** "I'm researching how top-performing electrical contractors handle growth pains. I'd love to get your perspective on managing overhead." (Owners love to share their "wisdom").

---

## 3. The Interview Script (The "Mom Test" Approach)

### Phase 1: The Warm Up (Build Trust)
*   "Thanks for chatting. I'm not selling anything today—I'm trying to solve a problem for a friend who runs an electrical shop."
*   "How long have you been in business? How big is the team now?"
*   "What's the biggest headache you're dealing with this month?" (Listen for: cash flow, hiring, materials).

### Phase 2: Digging for Pain (The Meat)
*   **The Leak:** "Walk me through the last time you paid a supplier invoice that looked 'off'. What happened? Did you catch it?"
*   **The Process:** "When a big project wraps up, how do you know if you actually made money on materials? Do you audit it?"
*   **The Maverick Spend:** "How do you handle it when a guy goes to Home Depot and buys a new drill on the company card without asking? Does that happen often?"
*   **The Cost:** "If you had to guess, what percentage of material purchases fall through the cracks each year? 1%? 5%?"

### Phase 3: Current Solutions (The Competition)
*   "Have you tried any software to fix this before? (e.g., Kojo, generic apps)."
*   "Why did you stop using them? Was it cost, or did the guys just refuse to use it?"
*   "How much time does your office admin spend chasing down receipts? Is that a frustration for you?"

### Phase 4: Pricing Psychology (Van Westendorp)
*   "If I could guarantee that every single purchase was matched to a job automatically:"
    *   "At what monthly price would this be a 'no-brainer' bargain?"
    *   "At what price would you say 'whoa, that's expensive' but still consider it?"
    *   "At what price would you say 'absolutely not'?"

## 4. Red Flags & Green Lights

| Signal | Meaning | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **"My guys are trustworthy, I don't worry about it."** | **RED FLAG.** They are either naive or small enough to manage by walking around. | **Disqualify.** Not a customer (yet). |
| **"I wish I could track it, but we're too busy."** | **YELLOW FLAG.** They feel the pain but might not prioritize fixing it. | **Keep warm.** |
| **"I lost $5k last month on a job because of unbilled materials."** | **GREEN LIGHT.** Acute pain. They are bleeding money. | **Primary Prospect.** |
| **"I tried Kojo/Procore but it was too complicated."** | **GREEN LIGHT.** They have *intent* to solve it but failed on *implementation*. Perfect for our "Invisible" app. | **Ideal Customer.** |

## 5. Post-Interview Analysis
*   **Did they mention a specific dollar amount lost?** (e.g., "$10,000/year").
*   **Did they mention a specific competitor they hate?**
*   **What is their "willingness to pay" threshold?**
